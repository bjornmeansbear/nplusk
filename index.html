The Brookshire Suites

We reserved a large block of rooms at a hotel right downtown. When you call for a reservation, please tell them you are part of the Iverson/Bjornard wedding block, they’ve got plenty of rooms saved for us. To make sure you get a room in our block and at our special rate please call them by September 12th.

The hotel is small enough that if you stay there you’ll pretty much only be with other wedding guests... pretty cool. It’s a new(ish) hotel, and it’s trying to be “gritty” and “baltimore-y.” Breakfast is served on the top floor where you can look out over the harbor — it’s a nice view. The inner harbor is close enough to walk to, you can buy tickets to the aquarium at the front desk, and it isn’t far from many of the other interesting things Baltimore has to offer.

Getting Into & Around Bmore

The closest airport is BWI — Baltimore Washington International. We recommend you try getting a flight into there. You can also fly into Reagan or Dulles in DC if necessary, though they are both rather far from Baltimore and are not easy to travel to unless you rent a car. You can get to downtown Baltimore from BWI by the lightrail or by taxi fairly simply.

For those of you coming from out of town, on Saturday we’re renting some busses so that you won’t have to find your own way to the party. The busses will pick you up right at the hotel, and then drop you back off there at the end of the night.
Easy peasy lemon squeezy.

Getting around Baltimore without a car can be tough, but here are your best bets from downtown:

  •   Zipcar: Zipcar offers a few locations around downtown.
  •   Uber: This car service/phone app lets you call a car from all over town and is usually pretty quick.
  •   Taxis: From downtown it is pretty easy to grab a cab somewhere else — but it can be nearly impossible to find a cab back afterward. If you take a cab, make sure you get a cab company's number so you can request pickup from where ever you end up. Baltimore cabs are pretty unreliable, so we have typically come to rely on Uber the few times we ever need a lift somewhere.
